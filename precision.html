Sampling & QA

Sampling and quality-control discussion in a business-facing format.

This page summarizes the checkpoints commonly discussed around inquiry intake, sample review, pre-production alignment and delivery control.

Working flow

Typical development sequence.

Buyers can use these steps to frame comments and prepare technical discussion.

1

Inquiry intake

Collect style images, quantity, target market and delivery timing before quotation discussion.

2

Sampling

Cross-check the sample against reference images, measurements and buyer comments.

3

Pre-production alignment

Confirm materials, workmanship points and packing instructions before bulk starts.

4

Inspection & delivery

Keep checkpoints visible so shipment timing and product consistency remain aligned.

Useful review inputs

  • Reference image or style number
  • Measurement points or fit comments
  • Fabric, trimming and packing notes

Why this page matters

Clear expectationsIt shows buyers what to prepare before sample comments start.
Less back-and-forthQuality checkpoints and timing expectations are presented earlier.
Better handoffThe process connects product selection to production discussion more naturally.
