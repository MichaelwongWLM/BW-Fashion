Sampling & quality control

Clearer sampling discussions with practical checkpoints

This page turns sample handling and production preparation into a simple buyer-facing flow, without the oversized hero and heavy page weight that slowed the earlier version.

Suggested working flow

Use the same structure from the first inquiry through sample review and production discussion.

1

Inquiry intake

Collect reference images, style numbers, quantity, target market, and delivery timing before quoting.

2

Sample development

Cross-check the sample against the agreed visual direction, measurements, and construction notes.

3

Pre-production alignment

Confirm materials, workmanship points, labeling, and packaging requirements before bulk starts.

4

Inspection & shipment

Keep final quality checkpoints visible so shipment timing is based on readiness rather than assumption.

Useful sample-review checklist

  • Reference image or style number attached
  • Target size set or measurement direction clarified
  • Fabric, trim, and workmanship notes listed clearly
  • Comment photos or annotated points prepared
  • Target timing for revised sample confirmed

Pre-production checkpoints

  • Approved sample or comment baseline confirmed
  • Labeling and packaging notes aligned
  • Inspection focus points listed in writing
  • Shipment timing linked to readiness, not assumption
  • Any destination-market compliance notes shared early
