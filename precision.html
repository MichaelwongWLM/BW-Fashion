Sampling & quality control

Clear development checkpoints reduce avoidable rework.

This page presents a straightforward sequence for inquiry intake, sample review, pre-production preparation and delivery control.

Suggested working flow

Use these steps when discussing a new style or adapting an existing reference.

1

Inquiry intake

Collect style images, target market, expected quantity and delivery window before quoting.

2

Sampling

Cross-check the sample against reference images, measurements and buyer comments.

3

Pre-production control

Confirm materials, workmanship points and packaging instructions before bulk starts.

4

Inspection & delivery

Keep quality checkpoints visible so shipment timing and product consistency stay aligned.

Information that helps speed up development

  • Reference image or style number.
  • Target fabric direction, if already known.
  • Expected order quantity and size range.
  • Target ship window or sales season.
  • Any market-specific requirement that affects workmanship or labeling.

Key control points

  • Confirm the style direction before sample development starts.
  • Record buyer comments clearly after each sample review round.
  • Align packaging and labeling instructions before bulk production.
  • Keep shipment timing tied to inspection readiness, not assumptions.
